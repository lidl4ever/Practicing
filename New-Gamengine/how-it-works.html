How It works


GAMEngine is a decentralized video game financing platform built on the public Bitmark property title blockchain. It unites Investors, Creators and Fans across the $108 billion dollar (USD) video games industry by giving all stakeholders access to a game’s revenue streams and granting them governance voice in shaping the platform itself, creating a true free market.

INVESTOR


Use the platform to access and finance video game projects, securing a share of a game’s future revenue streams.

CREATOR


Be free of the constraints of traditional video game publishing and secure alternative pathways to funding.

FAN


Directly impact the games you love by funding them or contributing your time and expertise to a project.


FUNDRAISING


When a Creator seeks funding for a game project, they submit an investment proposal to the platform. The GAMEngine community can review the feasibility of the project and the Creator has the option to revise the project based on community feedback and re-submit for further review. Once the project details and investment terms are finalized, the project enters the fundraising stage and Investors can finance the project.


INVESTING


When a Investor finances a project on GAMEngine, they transfer Bitcoin (BTC) or Ether (ETH) to the platform where the currency is held in an escrow account governed by smart contracts. If the project does not reach the fundraising goal set by the Creator, the funds are released back to the Investor. If the project is successfully funded, the Investor is issued a property title, or “bitmark”, for a portion of that game’s revenue stream. Funds are released to the Creator over time based on agreed upon milestones. Once a game is published, GAMEngine will automatically split and transfer revenue to Investors each month, paid in Bitcoin (BTC) or Ether (ETH).


ISSUANCE OF PROPERTY TITLE


All funds transferred by Investors to GAMEngine are automatically split and released to the Creator and the platform’s operational pool. Similarly, all revenue generated from a published game is automatically divvied between Creators, Investors and the operational pool.

SECONDARY MARKET


Investors can trade their bitmarks with other investors and gain liquidity within the GAMEngine secondary market. When these transactions take place, the platform will transfer the property title for the revenue stream to the new owner.
